NPD1241 Preceptorship Program for Nursing and Midwifery Staff
May 16th and 17th | CEWD Rozelle
What is a Preceptor?
Preceptorship is recognised as a vital component of providing support to nurses and midwives as they transition into a new work environment. It refers to the support and supervision of new nurses and midwives when they start in a clinical area, with the goal to provide safety for patients and staff at a time when an increased level of supervision is required.
Program Overview
Participants in this program will be provided with the opportunity to develop the knowledge, skills and attitudes required to be an effective preceptor. Sessions will cover topics that relate to preceptorship roles and responsibilities, clinical education and facilitation, socialisation, critical thinking and assessment.

Learning Objectives
  • Identify the roles, responsibilities and competencies of a preceptor in SLHD.
  • Reflect on the preceptor knowledge and skills you need to develop further and plan how you will develop them.
  • Reflect on how undergraduate and graduate nurses experience the transition process in order to better support, and identify/manage preceptee issues early.
  • Specify strategies a preceptor can use to aid socialization and to offer support to new staff.
  • Discuss teaching principles used as an effective preceptor.
  • Practice using effective communication skills when giving feedback and dealing with challenging preceptee situations.

Particpant Requirements
Participants are required to complete a two day face-to-face workshop. On completion of this training you will be expected to work towards becoming a preceptor for your ward/department.
